How It Works

Simple COLA

Draft order is determined by drought length: consecutive years without winning a playoff series or receiving a top-3 draft pick. Longest drought gets the #1 pick. Tiebreaker: most regular-season wins.

No lottery. Fully deterministic. Losses never improve your position.

Classic COLA

Each non-playoff team accumulates a lottery index (+1,000/year). Playoff success and high draft picks reduce the index. Picks 1-4 are drawn via weighted lottery (more index = better odds). Picks 5-14 by reverse standings.

Teams that have been bad the longest get the best odds, but outcomes are probabilistic.

Why COLA?

Munro & Banchio (2020) proved no single-season, record-based draft mechanism can simultaneously help weak teams and prevent tanking. COLA escapes this impossibility by using multi-year playoff track records instead of single-season records.

About This Tool

Uses actual NBA data from 1999-00 through 2024-25. COLA indices are computed using real playoff results and draft picks. Classic COLA shows probabilities (not deterministic outcomes) for picks 1-4.

COLA mechanism by Highley, Duncan & Volkov (2026).
