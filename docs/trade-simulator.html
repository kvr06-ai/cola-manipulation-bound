The Trade Problem

COLA (Carry-Over Lottery Allocation) is a proposed NBA draft reform that replaces single-season tank incentives with a multi-year system. Each non-playoff team accumulates "lottery tickets" over time. The longer you go without playoff success, the more tickets you hold — and the better your chances of landing a top draft pick. When you do win a top-4 pick, your ticket count is reduced, making room for other struggling teams.

This creates a design question around traded picks. If the Clippers trade a future pick to the Sixers and it becomes the #1 overall selection, whose ticket count should be reduced — the Clippers (who originally held the draft slot) or the Sixers (who benefited from the pick)?

Prof. Highley’s analysis identifies four options, each with different trade-offs. This tool computes the effect of each option across 26 seasons of real NBA history (63 traded lottery picks, 12 in the top 4 that trigger ticket reductions).
