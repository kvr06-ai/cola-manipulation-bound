Methodology

The 48 configurations vary three dials of the COLA design space: eligibility size E ∈ {14, 22, 16-tiered}, stockpile cap C ∈ {∞, 100, 150, 200}, and carry-over scope S ∈ {single-season, unbounded, bounded-30yr, reset-on-championship}. Each configuration is simulated for 50 replicates of 30 seasons; the real ZenGM lottery code is used unchanged, while regular-season outcomes are synthesized and team strength persists across seasons via a Markov model whose parameters are calibrated for plausibility (full parameters in paper §5.1). Sensitivity at N=30 and N=100 on the 9-config subset above confirms no flagged drift in the primary objective (mean shift ≤ 10% across replicate counts).

Three assumptions worth flagging

  1. Real ZenGM lottery; synthesized regular season. The ZenGM lottery code runs unchanged. Regular-season game outcomes are synthesized (strength-weighted wins + single-elim playoff bracket) because ZenGM's createLeague() does not port cleanly to Node. The synthesis bypasses contracts, trades, and injuries (none of which the dial space controls) but preserves the feedback loop the primary objective measures.
  2. Team strength is a Markov model calibrated for plausibility. Persistence, parity mean, per-draft impact, and annual shock variance were smoke-tuned so the CF-gap distribution disperses relative to an i.i.d. baseline. Parameters are not fit to historical NBA team-strength trajectories; sensitivity on these parameters is future work.
  3. Three dials swept; the other four held at Classic-COLA defaults. Eligibility (E), cap (C), and carry-over scope (S) span the 48-cell grid. Increment Δ, diminishment ρ, lottery weighting W, and tiebreak T are fixed; Δ scales the bound linearly (already in Theorem 1), and the others lack a published-policy anchor for sweeping.
